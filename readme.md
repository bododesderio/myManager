# MyManager

> **The all-in-one social media management platform for creators, brands, and agencies.**  
> Post once. Reach everywhere.

---

## Executive Summary

MyManager is a fully white-labelled, multi-tenant SaaS platform that enables individuals, brands, and agencies to create, schedule, publish, and analyse social media content across 10 platforms from a single interface — on web and mobile.

Built specifically for the African market with full Ugandan payment support (MTN MoMo, Airtel Money), multilingual support including Swahili and Arabic, and first-class Google Business Profile integration that no other African tool provides. Simultaneously built for global scale with enterprise team management, white-labelled client reporting, and a full agency operations layer.

The platform is entirely white-labelled at three levels — the platform itself can be rebranded as anything, agencies can use their own branding on client-facing materials, and every client project can carry the client's own logo and colors. Nothing — not even the copyright year — is hardcoded anywhere in the system.

## The Problem

### For individual creators and small businesses

Posting consistently to multiple social media platforms is time-consuming, repetitive, and uncoordinated. A creator typically has 4–6 active platforms, each with its own app, its own image size requirements, its own character limits, and its own posting schedule. They are constantly:

- Copying and pasting the same caption across apps, reformatting for each platform
- Forgetting to post on certain platforms entirely
- Losing track of what performed well and what didn't
- Losing hours per week to a task that should take minutes
- Posting at random times rather than when their audience is most active
- Having no unified view of their overall social media performance

### For social media agencies

Managing social media for multiple clients — each with their own platforms, their own brand voice, their own approval processes, and their own reporting expectations — is operationally complex:

- Content moves through email chains for approval, with no traceability
- Reports are manually assembled in PowerPoint each month
- Clients have no visibility into scheduled content without being sent screenshots
- Multiple tools are in use simultaneously (Canva, Buffer, Google Sheets, email) with no integration
- There is no unified view of all client performance in one place
- Billing clients and tracking retainer hours is managed outside the social tool entirely

### For the African market specifically

Most social media management tools are built for and priced for US and European markets. They:

- Don't support MTN MoMo or Airtel Money — the dominant payment methods across East Africa
- Don't support Swahili, the most widely spoken language in East Africa
- Don't have a Google Business Profile integration — critical for the millions of SMEs whose primary discovery channel is Google Maps
- Are priced at USD rates that are inaccessible for freelancers and small agencies earning in UGX, KES, or NGN
- Have no local customer support or community

## The Solution

MyManager solves all of these problems in one platform:

**For creators**: Write once, post everywhere. A single composer writes your caption, selects your platforms, attaches your media, shows you exactly how the post will look on each platform before it goes live, and publishes simultaneously with one tap. Analytics are consolidated — see your total reach, engagement rate, and top-performing content across all platforms on one dashboard.

**For agencies**: Full team and client management. Each client is a "project" with their own connected social accounts, their own brand identity, and their own approval workflow. Content goes from draft → team review → client approval → published without leaving the platform. A white-labelled client portal gives clients real-time visibility. Branded PDF reports are generated automatically every month.

**For African markets**: Full MTN MoMo and Airtel Money payment support via Flutterwave (licensed by the Bank of Uganda). Swahili and Arabic language support. Google Business Profile posting integration. Pricing in local currency (UGX, KES, NGN) with automatic conversion.

## Who Uses MyManager

### Creator / Individual (Free, Starter, Pro)

A blogger, photographer, musician, influencer, or entrepreneur managing their own social presence. They use MyManager to save hours every week on content distribution, get analytics they can actually act on, and grow their audience consistently across platforms.

Example: A Kampala-based fashion photographer who posts daily to Instagram, X, TikTok, and Facebook. Currently spending 45 minutes per day copying captions and re-uploading images to each app separately.

### Brand / SME (Pro)

A business with a marketing team of one or two people managing the brand's social channels. They need scheduling, post performance data, best time recommendations, and the ability to plan a month of content in advance.

Example: A Nairobi restaurant that posts daily specials to Facebook, Google Business Profile, WhatsApp channels, and Instagram. Needs to schedule content in batches and track which specials drive the most engagement.

### Social Media Agency (Enterprise)

An agency managing social media for 5–20 client brands. They need multi-user team management, per-client projects with separate social account connections, approval workflows, white-labelled client reports, and the ability to onboard new clients smoothly.

Example: A Kampala-based digital marketing agency with 4 team members managing 8 clients. Needs to assign team members to specific clients, get client approval on posts before publishing, and send each client a branded monthly performance report.

### Platform Reseller (Custom)

An agency or SaaS company that wants to offer social media management software under their own brand to their own clients. They white-label MyManager completely — different name, different logo, different domain, potentially their own branded mobile app.

## User Journeys

### Journey 1: New creator signs up (Free plan)

1. Creator visits the landing page, reads the features, and clicks "Get started free"
2. Signs up with email or Google. Email verified.
3. Lands on plan selection: Free is pre-selected. They skip to continue.
4. Sets up profile: display name, avatar, timezone (auto-detected).
5. Connects social accounts: two slots shown (Free plan limit). They connect Facebook and Instagram via OAuth popups. Both tick green "Connected."
6. A guided overlay walks them through the composer: write a caption, select platforms, attach an image, preview it on Facebook and Instagram, tap "Post now."
7. Live status card shows: "Facebook ✓ Posted" and "Instagram ✓ Posted" within seconds.
8. They land on the dashboard. A sidebar checklist shows "Connect all 4 platforms", "Schedule your first post", "View analytics." These items are dismissed as they complete them.
9. Every week they get a Sunday email: "Your week in review — 12 posts, 8.4K total reach."

### Journey 2: Creator upgrades from Free to Pro

1. Creator hits the 4-post monthly limit on day 18 of the month. The Post button shows "You've used 4/4 posts this month. Upgrade to continue posting."
2. They click the upgrade prompt. A Flutterwave checkout modal opens: they select MTN MoMo, enter their number, approve the UGX 72,000 charge on their phone.
3. Pro activates immediately. The Post button unlocks. They now see 3 additional social account slots, unlimited posts, and the scheduling calendar.
4. They get a welcome to Pro email with their invoice attached.

### Journey 3: Agency creates a project and onboards a client

1. Company Owner (on Enterprise) creates a new project: "Nike Uganda."
2. Opens project settings → Brand: uploads Nike's logo, sets the brand primary color to #FF6600.
3. Goes to Client onboarding → "Send onboarding link." Nike's marketing contact receives a branded email (Nike logo visible) asking them to connect their social accounts and fill in brand guidelines.
4. Nike's contact connects Facebook Page and Instagram Business account via OAuth. No login to MyManager — the onboarding link handles everything.
5. Agency team member (Sarah) is assigned to the Nike Uganda project by the Company Owner.
6. Sarah writes a post in the Nike Uganda project composer. She submits for approval. The Company Owner reviews it in the Approvals section, leaves a comment "Change the hashtag to #JustDoItUganda", and requests revision. Sarah edits and resubmits. The Owner approves. The post is scheduled.
7. Nike's contact receives an email with their client portal link. They open it on mobile — see Nike's own logo, colors, and "Prepared by Acme Agency" in the footer. They see 3 posts pending their approval. They tap Approve on all three. The agency is notified in real-time.
8. On March 1st, a white-labelled PDF report is automatically generated and emailed to Nike's contact. It shows the previous month's performance with Nike branding. The agency never had to manually create a report.

### Journey 4: Posting to TikTok and Google Business Profile

1. Creator opens composer. In the platform selector, they tap TikTok and Google Business Profile alongside their usual Facebook and Instagram.
2. For TikTok: a vertical video preview appears. They see their caption in TikTok's style. Below: Privacy (Public), Allow comments (on), Allow duet (off). The character counter shows 180/2200.
3. For Google Business: a separate section appears. Post type: "What's New" (selected). They see a Google Search card preview showing how the post appears in Google Maps results for their business.
4. They tap "Post now." Four publishing jobs fire in parallel. Within 2 minutes: Facebook ✓, Instagram ✓, TikTok (Processing — usually 1–5 minutes for video encoding), Google Business ✓.
5. 3 minutes later, a notification: "TikTok ✓ Posted."

## Feature Overview

### How Billing Works

**Payment methods** (Uganda and global):
- MTN Mobile Money: USSD push to phone, approve with MoMo PIN, instant
- Airtel Money: USSD push, identical flow to MTN
- Visa / Mastercard: any local or international card
- Google Pay (Android)
- Apple Pay (iPhone, Safari on Mac)
- Bank transfer (virtual account, 24h settlement)

**Currency display**: Prices shown in user's detected local currency (UGX, KES, GBP, EUR, USD, NGN, etc.) with live exchange rate conversion from USD master prices. User can change currency in settings.

**Subscription renewal**: Auto-renews on the same date each month (or year for annual). The currency and converted amount are locked at signup — exchange rate changes don't affect renewal amounts.

**Failed payment**: 3 Flutterwave retry attempts over 7 days. Features remain active during retry window. On final failure: plan downgrades to Free. Data is never deleted.

**Downgrade**: Scheduled at end of billing period — no immediate cutoff. Posts, accounts, and team members beyond the new plan's limits are soft-locked (visible, not deletable, but unpostable) until the user either upgrades or removes the excess.

**Enterprise seat billing**: Seats billing starts on acceptance of the invitation (pending invites don't count). Removing a seat triggers a prorated credit on the next invoice.

**Manual override**: Superadmin can assign any plan to any user for any duration without touching Flutterwave (for trials, compensations, partners). All overrides logged with reason and admin ID.

## Technical Summary

### Architecture Style
Turborepo monorepo · 4 apps (web+admin, mobile, api) · 7 shared packages · 100% TypeScript throughout

### Backend
NestJS REST API · PostgreSQL (Prisma ORM) · Redis · BullMQ for all async jobs · 20 worker processes · 8 cron jobs

### Publishing
Platform-agnostic worker pattern · Each platform is a separate BullMQ queue and worker file · Parallel publishing: all selected platforms fire simultaneously · Independent retry per platform (one failure doesn't affect others) · Real-time status via Socket.io

### Data Model
~60 PostgreSQL tables · Platform definitions and limits are data (not code) · Plan definitions are data (not code) · BrandConfig is data (not code) · Translations are data (not code) · Superadmin can adjust any of these without a code deployment

### Hosting
Web app + Admin dashboard: Vercel (web and admin route group deployed together, zero-config Next.js, edge middleware, global CDN)
API + Workers: Railway (managed PostgreSQL, Redis, Docker)
Media: Cloudflare R2 (zero egress fees, S3-compatible)
All traffic: Cloudflare (CDN, DDoS, IP geolocation, DNS)

### Mobile
Expo React Native SDK 53 (iOS + Android from one codebase) · EAS Build for App Store/Play Store deployment · OTA updates for JS changes (no app store re-review) · Share extension (iOS + Android)

### Security
JWT + refresh tokens · AES-256 encrypted OAuth tokens · CSRF protection on all OAuth flows · PostgreSQL RLS for multi-tenant isolation · HMAC-SHA256 on all webhooks · TOTP 2FA · Append-only audit log · GDPR compliant
